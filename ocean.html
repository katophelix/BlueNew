Ocean Fishing

The State regulates fishing up to three mile offshore. Beyond that, Federal regulations apply.
For salt water fishing you need a State license if you are over 16 years old.
To fish from the beach or the banks of estuaries (tidal creeks) or a dock or pier you now need a license. You cannot fish in designated swimming areas and you are not supposed to fish for sharks. The sharks break this rule all the time, so you might hook a Sting Ray or Atlantic Sharp Nosed or Black Tipped shark. You are expected to release them. If you get a Sting Ray or a good size shark, just cut the line and let it go rather than take a chance of getting hurt.
Inshore and offshore Blue Water charter fishing boats are readily available.
You can go fishing on a boat with a dozen or more people (The Hobo can hold 16 people). You can also charter an offshore (or inshore) boat that carries up to six passengers.
The charters can accommodate several differ types of fishing.
They can drift fish (fishing with the boat not moving), fish by trolling the bait, (dragging it behind a moving boat). This is usually called deep sea fishing. They can even focus on Sharks. Shark fishing is usually late in the day and into evening.
The trips offered are typically either half-day or full-day excursions.
The Charters can stay inshore, go a little off shore or go to the Gulf Stream.
For serious deep sea fishing, anglers will go to the Gulf Stream, which is about 70 miles from shore. The fishing is great, but so is the round trip. Obviously a very full day of fishing. The best time to go to the Gulf Stream is June through September.
There are a number of artificial reefs and natural banks offshore that offer fine fishing. The Blue Water charter captains know where to go at various times of year and can predict the types of fish that may be caught.
Our recommendation for charter fishing is to go to the docks or call and talk to the charter boat captains to see what is a good fit for you. Blue Water charters (843) 671-3060.
